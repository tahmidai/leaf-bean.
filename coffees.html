Types of Coffee

From bean to brew, coffee offers an incredible range of flavors influenced by origin, processing, roasting, and preparation methods.

Arabica

Arabica beans account for about 60-70% of global coffee production. Grown at higher altitudes (2,000-6,000 feet), they have a sweeter, softer taste with notes of sugar, fruit, and berries. Lower in caffeine than Robusta.

Flavor profile: Smooth, sweet, complex with fruity or floral notes

Popular origins: Ethiopia, Colombia, Brazil, Guatemala

Caffeine: 1.2-1.5% by weight

Robusta

Robusta beans have higher caffeine content and a stronger, more bitter flavor. They're easier to cultivate, more disease-resistant, and yield more crops per plant. Often used in espresso blends and instant coffee.

Flavor profile: Strong, bitter, nutty, with chocolate notes

Popular origins: Vietnam, Brazil, Indonesia, Uganda

Caffeine: 2.2-2.7% by weight

Espresso Family

Not a bean type but a brewing method that produces concentrated coffee. Espresso forms the base for many popular drinks:

  • Espresso: 1oz concentrated coffee
  • Americano: Espresso + hot water
  • Cappuccino: Equal parts espresso, steamed milk, foam
  • Latte: Espresso with more steamed milk and light foam
  • Macchiato: Espresso "stained" with a dash of milk

Brew Methods

Different brewing methods extract coffee flavors uniquely:

  • Pour Over: Clean, bright flavor (V60, Chemex)
  • French Press: Full-bodied, rich with oils
  • Aeropress: Smooth, low acidity, versatile
  • Cold Brew: Smooth, low acidity, sweet (12-24 hour steep)
  • Moka Pot: Strong, concentrated, espresso-like

Coffee Culture

The Third Wave Coffee Movement

The third wave coffee movement treats coffee as an artisanal product rather than a commodity. It emphasizes:

  • Direct trade: Building relationships with farmers
  • Light roasting: Preserving bean's origin characteristics
  • Precise brewing: Attention to grind size, water temperature, and brew time
  • Single-origin: Highlighting unique regional flavors
  • Transparency: Knowing the farm, altitude, and processing method

This movement has transformed coffee from a simple caffeine delivery system into a complex, nuanced beverage enjoyed for its flavor profiles much like wine.
