Brewing Guide

Master the art of perfect extraction with our comprehensive brewing tips and techniques.

Essential Brewing Principles

Great brewing is about controlling variables to extract the best flavors from your tea leaves or coffee beans. Follow these guidelines for consistently excellent results.

Tea Brewing Basics

Water Temperature: Different teas require different temperatures. Green teas (160-180°F), black teas (200-212°F), oolongs (185-205°F).

Steeping Time: Typically 2-5 minutes. Over-steeping releases bitter tannins.

Tea Quantity: 1 teaspoon per 8oz cup, adjust to taste.

Water Quality: Use filtered water for best results. Hard water can affect flavor.

Coffee Brewing Basics

Grind Size: Match grind to method: coarse for French press, medium for drip, fine for espresso.

Water Temperature: 195-205°F (90-96°C) for optimal extraction.

Coffee-to-Water Ratio: 1:15 to 1:18 ratio (e.g., 20g coffee to 300g water).

Brew Time: Varies by method: 4 minutes for French press, 2-3 minutes for pour over.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Tea Brewing Mistakes

  • Boiling water for green tea: Scorches delicate leaves, causing bitterness
  • Over-steeping: Releases excessive tannins, making tea bitter
  • Using low-quality water: Chlorine or minerals mask delicate flavors
  • Reusing tea bags too many times: Second steep is usually fine, but flavor diminishes quickly
  • Storing tea improperly: Tea absorbs odors; store in airtight containers away from light

Coffee Brewing Mistakes

  • Inconsistent grind size: Leads to uneven extraction
  • Using stale beans: Coffee peaks 7-14 days after roasting
  • Incorrect water temperature: Too hot burns coffee, too cold under-extracts
  • Not measuring: Inconsistent ratios lead to inconsistent flavor
  • Neglecting equipment cleaning: Oils build up and become rancid

Pro Tips for Advanced Brewing

Tea Pro Tips

  • Pre-warm your teapot: Rinse with hot water before brewing to maintain temperature
  • Try gongfu style: Multiple short steeps (20-30 seconds) for oolongs and pu-erh
  • Experiment with water: Different mineral contents can highlight different flavor notes
  • Understand "agony of the leaves": Watch tea leaves unfurl—it's part of the experience
  • Match teaware to tea: Porcelain for delicate greens, clay for roasted oolongs

Coffee Pro Tips

  • Weigh your coffee: Volume measurements are inconsistent due to bean density variations
  • Blooming: For fresh coffee, pour just enough water to saturate grounds and wait 30 seconds before continuing
  • Control pour rate: For pour over, maintain a steady, slow pour for even extraction
  • Experiment with agitation: Stirring at different stages affects extraction
  • Temperature drop technique: For some methods, starting hotter and allowing temperature to drop can extract different flavor compounds
